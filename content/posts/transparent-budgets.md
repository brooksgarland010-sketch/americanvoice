Accountability begins with clear information. When budgets are locked in PDFs or hidden behind jargon, people disengage. A transparent process means residents can see how decisions are made and how priorities shift over time.

## A practical framework

1. Publish a public-facing summary within 72 hours of approval.
2. Provide line-item data in a downloadable format.
3. Annotate changes with plain-language explanations.

## Why this matters

Transparency does not require perfect agreement. It requires consistent access to facts, decisions, and follow-through. That is the standard we should expect in every public institution.
